Fitness Tips

One of the best things you can do for your health is exercise. Physical activity has major benefits on your body both physically and mentally. however it requires a lot of discipline and determination to fit it into your routine and to keep at it in the long run. Exercise is proven to significantly improve your health, it helps maintain a healthy body, gain muscle mass and reduce risk of chronic disease. Research has shown that exercise has a huge effect on boosting your mental health, can help lift your mood, helps you get a good night's rest and can even enhance your sex life. These are the main reasons that exercising and staying fit can change your life.- Jack



Getting started!

When starting note you should always make a plan and set realistic goals, Don't try and act tough you just end up injuring yourself. you should start a small plan with a few exercises every day comma and when you can feel your fitness increasing you can add on to your plan. The main thing is you should never give up, if you think that's all you have to give your own, just push that bit harder and you will see what you can really do. The key to success is to make it a habit and plan a routine every day. Its good to have a rest day or even 2 rest days a week. Resting your muscles is a huge part of keeping fit. Here is a sample week to get you started:

  • Monday- 35-40 minute walk or light jog.
  • Tuesday- Rest day.
  • Wednesday- Do a light 5min walk. Complete these 2 circuits with a 1 minute rest between each set.
    • Circuit 1: 2 sets of 20 lunges, 10 sit-ups and 10 press-ups
    • Circuit 2: 2 sets of 10 squats, 10 bench-dips and 10 burpees
  • Thursday- Rest day.
  • Friday- Go for a 35min run.
  • Saturday- Rest day.
  • Sunday- Go for a 35-40min run, walk or jog.

Health Food tips:

During isolation it can be hard to be motivated to eat healthy and carry out meal prep, it can be easy to fall into bad habits. Follow these tips to remain healthy during this time:
  1. Add Herbs
    Culinary herbs are leafy plants that add flavour and colour to all types of meals. They are also rich in health-protective phytoestrogens. (help prevent bone loss in aging) In many cases, herbs can replace the flavour of salt and oil. Herbs are lightly flavoured, so add them to your cooking in the last few minutes.
  2. Cutting down salt
    Salt is a common flavour enhancer, but research suggests that a high salt diet could contribute to a range of health problems including high blood pressure. Don’t automatically add salt to your food , taste it first as this is a bad habit.
  3. Low fat cooking
    If you need to use oil, try cooking sprays or apply a small amount of oil with a pastry brush. Cook in liquids (such as stock, wine, lemon juice, vinegar or water) instead of oil.
  4. fats to a minimum
    If you add fats when cooking, keep them to a minimum and use monounsaturated oils such as olive oil. This recipe is SimplyHealth approved and is quick to make with worthy results. Potato and Green Bean salad: A nice idea for your lunch and easy to prepare , ready in 45 minutes. What You Need:
  • 1 1/2 pounds red potatoes
  • 3/4-pound fresh green beans, trimmed and snapped
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil
  • 1 small red onion, chopped
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 dash Worcestershire sauce
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil

Place the potatoes in a large pot, and fill with about 1 inch of water. Bring to a boil, and cook for about 15 minutes, or until potatoes are tender. Throw in the green beans to steam after the first 10 minutes. Drain, cool, and cut potatoes into quarters. Transfer to a large bowl, and toss with fresh basil, red onion, salt and pepper. Set aside.
